History

Established

Devine was established in 1881 because the Great Northern Railroad extended from San Antonio to Laredo. The town was renamed after Judge Thomas Jefferson Devine. He was attorney and jurist for the railroad.

Total Area

3.1 Square miles all of it land

Population

Devine began with a population of 505 people in 1904 when it was incorporated. From 1915 to about 1940 the population stayed at about 1,000 people. It rose to 1,400 in 1941 and in 1988 to 4,016. In 1990 Devine's population was 3,928. By 2000 the poplulation has grown to 4,140. Today the population stands at 4,927 people.

Fun Facts

Devine News orginally called the "Devine Wide Awake" was established in 1897 by WL DuBose. It is still owned and operated by Kathleen(KK) Dubose Calame and her daughter Kayleen Holder(editor) who are the fourth and fifth generations respectively. It is the oldest business in Devine still in operation.

In 1906 the Opera House was built. It was at the center of town and was a place for Entertaining. In 1975 was placed in the National Registry of Historical Places under the Historic Preservation Act of 1966.

Stroud's Blacksmith Shop still stands, and is the oldest blacksmith shop in Texas. "This shop became known throughout the Southwest for Stroud's branding irons and for Stroud's reputation as an expert farrier." "He was know to shod all four feet of a horse in nine minutes."
